What is SMIRC?

SMIRC (Short Message Internet Relay Chat) is a fusion of Short Message Service (also known as "SMS" or "texting" to most any user of a cellphone) and Internet Relay Chat. A typical SMS (text message) sent from a phone is targeted to a single recipient and although phones are getting smarter and better able to send multi-recipient text messages the ability to have a multi-person conversation (as is common in e-mail) is still largely missing. SMIRC's goal is to expose this functionality to it's users, so that they are able to quickly and easily have multi-recipient conversations. On SMIRC, therefore, a message is targeted at a room of people, rather than at a person.

How does it work? What does it look like?

An example of how SMIRC can work: let's assume that there are three friends (we'll call them Zim, Gir, and Tallest) who want to have conversations through text messaging. Typically, each of them would need to make very sure that they always listed all of the recipients for each text message that they sent... and if Gir (for example) hit "reply" to a text message from Zim and forgot to add Tallest as a recipient, then only Zim would receive the message and Tallest might be out of the loop. Of course the Tallest won't stand for this, so all three join SMIRC and find that it can make things easier:
